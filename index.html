Pendleton Chapel Seminary

Pendleton Chapel Seminary forms disciples, pastors, and chaplains for faithful service in churches, communities, and public spaces. Rooted in Baptist tradition and shaped by chaplaincy practice, we prepare leaders to serve with peace, integrity, and wisdom.

Affiliation reflects shared heritage and cooperation; Pendleton Chapel Seminary is not governed by the Southern Baptist Convention or the Baptist General Convention of Texas.

Affiliation

Pendleton Chapel Seminary maintains longstanding connections within Southern Baptist life and the Baptist General Convention of Texas. Ordination and commissioning remain under local-church authority.

Pendleton Chapel Seminary is an independent religious institution rooted in Baptist tradition. Accreditation status, if applicable, is stated explicitly.

The Order of Melchizedek (Biblical Pattern)

We teach the Order of Melchizedek as a biblical pattern of peace, blessing, and nourishment—fulfilled in Jesus Christ (Hebrews 7). This framework informs our approach to leadership and chaplaincy in a Christ-centered and accountable way.

The Order of Melchizedek is taught as a biblical pattern fulfilled in Christ and does not confer priestly authority or replace local-church governance.

Peace

Ministry that stabilizes chaos through presence, truth, and reconciliation.

Blessing

Service offered without coercion, honoring dignity and conscience.

Nourishment

Grounding care, prayer when invited, and meaningful presence.
