Governance & Integrity

Pendleton Chapel Seminary

Pendleton Chapel Seminary operates under local governance with accountability to its board and supporting churches. The seminary affirms Baptist principles of congregational autonomy and shared leadership.

Affiliation reflects shared heritage and cooperation; Pendleton Chapel Seminary is not governed by the Southern Baptist Convention or the Baptist General Convention of Texas.

Governance Structure

Board Accountability

The seminary maintains governing documents, policies, and oversight practices appropriate to its size and mission.

  • Board of Directors: [List names] or “Available upon request”
  • Bylaws: maintained and updated as needed
  • Financial stewardship and ethical standards

Meeting Procedure

Meetings are conducted in an orderly, transparent manner.

  • Robert’s Rules of Order Newly Revised (unless bylaws provide otherwise)
  • Minutes recorded for major decisions
  • Conflict-of-interest awareness

Integrity Commitments

  • Truth in representation: Programs, credentials, and partnerships are described accurately.
  • Student safety: Policies support ethical ministry, healthy boundaries, and appropriate supervision.
  • Local-church authority: Ordination and commissioning remain governed by local churches.
  • Public ministry ethics: Chaplaincy training emphasizes non-coercive care, respect, and professional standards.

Accreditation Statement

Accreditation status, if applicable, is stated explicitly and verifiably. If the seminary pursues accreditation in the future, that information will be published with the accrediting body’s name and scope.

Melchizedek note: The Order of Melchizedek is taught as a biblical pattern fulfilled in Christ and does not confer priestly authority or replace local-church governance.
