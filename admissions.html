Admissions & Master of Divinity (MDiv)

Pendleton Chapel Seminary

Pendleton Chapel Seminary welcomes students seeking preparation for pastoral ministry, chaplaincy, and advanced discipleship. Our admissions process emphasizes calling, character, and readiness for formation in both church and public ministry contexts.

Pendleton Chapel Seminary is an independent religious institution rooted in Baptist tradition. Ordination and commissioning are governed by local churches. Accreditation status, if applicable, is stated explicitly.

Master of Divinity (MDiv)

The Master of Divinity is a professional graduate degree designed to prepare students for pastoral leadership, chaplaincy, teaching, and community ministry. The program integrates biblical and theological study with spiritual formation and practical ministry skills.

Program Emphases

  • Biblical studies and Christian theology
  • Baptist polity and ecclesiology
  • Pastoral leadership and preaching/teaching
  • Chaplaincy foundations and ethics
  • Supervised ministry and formation

Who the MDiv Serves

  • Pastors and church leaders
  • Chaplains (hospital, hospice, correctional, community)
  • Bi-vocational ministers
  • Mission and nonprofit leaders

Admissions Requirements

Requirements

  • Bachelor’s degree or ministry-equivalent experience
  • Personal statement of calling and goals
  • Church or ministry reference
  • Admissions interview

Background checks may be required for supervised ministry or chaplaincy placements.
