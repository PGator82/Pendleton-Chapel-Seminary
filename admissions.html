Admissions & MDiv

Our admissions process is designed to confirm calling, readiness, and character—not just coursework completion. The MDiv pathway supports discipleship, pastoral leadership, and chaplaincy preparation.

Who We Admit

Disciples

Students pursuing formation, biblical grounding, and ministry basics.

Pastoral Leaders

Students preparing for teaching, preaching, shepherding, and church leadership.

Chaplains

Students preparing for ethical spiritual care in public and institutional settings.

Admissions Requirements

  • Application form (Google Form)
  • Statement of calling and ministry goals
  • Pastor / ministry reference (as applicable)
  • Interview (online or in-person)

Note: Ordination and ecclesial commissioning are governed by local churches.

MDiv Overview

Core categories (sample structure):

  • Biblical Studies (OT/NT survey, hermeneutics)
  • Theology (systematic, Baptist distinctives, ethics)
  • Ministry Practice (preaching, teaching, leadership)
  • Pastoral Care (counseling basics, grief, crisis)
  • Field Education / Practicum (church or chaplaincy setting)
