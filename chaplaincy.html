Chaplaincy Track

The Chaplaincy Track trains students to provide non-coercive spiritual care that honors patient/client autonomy, religious diversity, confidentiality, and ethical boundaries in institutional settings.

Completion of chaplaincy coursework does not guarantee certification or employment and does not replace requirements set by endorsing bodies or employers.

Competencies (Practical)

Spiritual Assessment

Listen, identify needs, and document respectfully using objective language.

Crisis & Grief Care

Support patients/families through trauma, loss, and end-of-life moments.

Ethics & Boundaries

Consent, confidentiality, non-proselytizing posture, and referral practices.

Possible Settings

  • Hospital / hospice
  • Corrections / re-entry support
  • Community chaplaincy
  • Disaster / crisis response
