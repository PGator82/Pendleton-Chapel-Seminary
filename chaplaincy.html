Chaplaincy Track

Pendleton Chapel Seminary

The Chaplaincy Track prepares students for ministry in healthcare, correctional, educational, and community settings where spiritual care must be offered with humility, ethics, and respect for diversity.

Clinical posture: Students are trained to provide non-coercive spiritual care that honors patient autonomy, cultural and religious diversity, confidentiality, and ethical boundaries within interdisciplinary teams.

Core Competencies

Spiritual Care Skills

  • Spiritual assessment and care planning
  • Crisis, grief, and end-of-life support
  • Meaning-making and dignity-centered presence
  • Prayer/ritual when invited and appropriate

Professional Practice

  • Professional boundaries, consent, and confidentiality
  • Interfaith engagement and cultural humility
  • Interdisciplinary communication and referrals
  • Clinical documentation in objective, respectful language

Training Topics

Trauma-Informed Care

  • Trauma awareness and stabilization
  • Moral injury and grief
  • De-escalation and grounding practices

Ethics & Policy Alignment

  • Non-proselytizing care in public institutions
  • Respect for institutional policies and documentation expectations
  • Safe escalation and reporting boundaries when required

Important Clarification

Completion of chaplaincy coursework does not guarantee certification or employment and does not replace requirements set by endorsing bodies or employers. Students are responsible for meeting requirements established by certifying bodies, endorsing organizations, and employers.

Governance note: Ordination and commissioning are governed by local churches. Pendleton Chapel Seminary is an independent institution rooted in Baptist tradition.
